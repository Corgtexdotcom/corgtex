Articles are the native wiki aspect of the Organization Brain. They act as your organization's internal handbook, ideal for qualitative, narrative content that doesn't belong in a raw data export.

## When to use Articles

Because Corgtex can automatically sync with your databases and files, you shouldn't need to manually write down everything your company does. 

Use Articles for:
- **Core Methodology:** Manifestos, guiding principles, or company values.
- **Narrative context:** Detailed onboarding guides that provide the "why" behind the "what".
- **Living documents:** Collaborative policy drafts taking place in [proposals](/decide/proposals).

## Viewing and editing

Navigate to the **Brain** section of your workspace to view the article index.

### Version history
Corgtex tracks every update to an article. If an article goes through an approval flow, the versions are securely tracked, providing a clear audit trail of who changed exactly what and when.

### Discussions
Unlike static wikis, team members can start discussion threads directly on an article. This allows for questions, clarifications, and collaborative improvement without polluting the official document text.

## How Agents use Articles

Articles are given high priority weighting during [Search and Ask](/know/search-and-ask) queries. Because they represent human-curated truth, the AI agents will strongly anchor their responses to the content of your published articles when resolving context.
